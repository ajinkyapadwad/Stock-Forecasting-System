Red flags going up across markets, signal possible pullback for stocks

Article from CNBC  •  Filed under templates, internet

Markets have been raising some red flags this week that analysts say could result in increased volatility and possibly a stock market pullback in the near term. Driven by worries about North Korea, Syria and the French election, investors have been loading up on safe haven plays and lightening up on risk. One of the big concerns of the week has been the fact that a North Korean missile launch is speculated to come this weekend to commemorate its Day of the Sun, a holiday honoring the birth of its founder, Kim Il Sung.

The launch was expected to be in the middle of a three-day Easter holiday weekend, and traders have been hedging ahead of it. Adding to the nervousness, the U.S. dropped the largest ever non-nuclear bomb in Afghanistan during market hours Thursday, and on Thursday night, NBC News reported that U.S. officials were planning a possible pre-emptive strike. While the market moves haven't been deep, they have been surgical. Many of them are technically significant or a return to levels last seen right around the election. The stock market is still up sharply, with the S&P 500 having risen more than 9 percent since Election Day.
